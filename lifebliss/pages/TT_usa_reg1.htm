The Roles and Responsibilities

“They Display the same confidence to the world that I display”

-Nithyananda

The Ananda Acharyas, the Teachers are disciples who live the teachings of the master and his philosophy. Acharyas express their understanding and personal experience by radiating the master’s philosophy in every step of their life. The acharyas imbibe the master’s identity in knowledge, thought and action. The Acharyas represent the Master’s identity to spread the message and reach out to the devotees. An Acharya becomes the Master’s spokes person and the spiritual ambassador and play the vital role of revealing the Master’s message to the world.

 

The Ananda Pramukhas are organizers who cognize projects to implement the Master’s vision in their centers.  The Pramukhas are devotees, who have experienced the benefits of Master’s teachings in their life, and express their gratitude to the Master by sharing their resources for the mission activities. A Pramukha takes the complete responsibility for running the programs, manage the local mission activities and ensure successful execution of the local center activities.

 

The Ananda Sevaks are fulltime volunteers, who have dedicated their life to service of the master’s mission. The Sevaks are disciples, who are completely in love with the Master’s mission and have chosen to serve the mission as their spiritual path. For all practical purposes, a Sevak is an ashramite in waiting. Seeking nothing for themselves but the pleasure of living the Master’s teachings, a Sevak is an important bridge between the outer world and the Ashram.

 

The Ananda Karya Kartas are part time volunteers. The Karya Kartas are devotees who have been deeply touched by the master. Having experienced direct benefits to their personal life, Karya Kartas express gratitude to the master by taking part in the mission activities. Karya Kartas are the back bone of the mission in reaching out to the public and are prime ambassadors of Master’s message to the world. The Karya Kartas simply radiate the truth of master’s promises of outer well being, by realizing them in their life. Karya Kartas contribute to the mission with valuable resources and execute the programs offered by the mission in their local centers.

 

The Ananda Samajis are the congregation, the devotees who are completely inspired by the philosophy and teachings of Swamiji and identify themselves with the Dharma. A Samajite feels that the welfare of Dharma is their prime responsibility and lend their support financially, morally and dharmically. The Samajites contribute 10% of their each earnings and other resources and take complete responsibility to make Mission realize its Vision.
