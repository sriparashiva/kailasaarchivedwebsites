   Medical Camps

Project Brief | Desired Range of Services | Future Needs | Pictures | Video

At 30 years Lakshmi amma looks aged beyond her years. She and her husband work as manual laborers at a construction site, each earning Rs.100 per day. The hard work that they do under the hot sun day in and day out while feeding and taking care of their 4 children leaves them in bad health most of the time. With barely any money to feed themselves, doctor visits, checkups and medicines are beyond their reach. This is the story of many such Lakshmi amma's coming from families living below the poverty level. 


PROJECT BRIEF

Dhyanapeeta Charitable Trust has been conducting various general and specialty medical camps in villages and towns in the South Indian states of Karnataka, Tamil Nadu and Andhra Pradesh and has covered about 30,000 people so far. The Mission wishes to expand both the range of these services and people reached to over 150,000 people. This is planned to be done by creating additional locations, a mobile dispensary and hi-tech ambulance in the villages around Bidadi in the states of Karnataka and Tamil Nadu. Also the medical camps will provide additional medical services like advanced diagnostics including scans, gynecological tests, ECG, sugar test, etc. 

SCOPE OF CURRENT SERVICES

Medical camps are being organized weekly at different places in Bidadi, Hyderabad, Namakkal  and Tiruvannamalai. These include general medical camps, specialty medical camps that include Acupressure, Ophthalmology, gynecology, ENT, artificial limb donation camps, AIDS awareness camps. thyroid awareness camps, diabetes control with free medicines etc as well as complementary systems of medicine such as Ayurveda, homeopathy, naturopathy and siddha systems of medicine.

To date we have conducted around 80 medical camps.  We have a dedicated team of around 10 doctors who are volunteers committed to providing and expanding the medical service.

  • Free general medical camps, touched about 30000 people so far

  • Specialty camps in the areas of gynecology, ophthalmology etc using allopathy as well as complementary systems of medicine like homeopathy, ayurveda, siddha, acupuncture etc.  

Along with the conventional means of healing through medicine, Dhyanapeeta Charitable Trust also offers complementary systems of ayurveda and siddha, the medicines of which are prepared by the experts in the respective fields under stringent conditions and have been approved by the regulatory authorities.

We have a group of doctors in the various specialties who offer their services to the medical camps. To extend the service, we plan to engage more such qualified doctors who will provide consultation and free medicines. 

As a part of World Aids Day, Nithyananda Youth Centre conducted the AIDS Awareness Camp at Viveka Matriculation School, Sendamangalam, South India
