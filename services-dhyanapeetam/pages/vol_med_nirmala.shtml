A Doctor with a Mission 
- Dr. Nirmala MBBS, DNB (Obs & Gynec)

Project Brief | Desired Range of Services | Future Needs | Pictures | Video

Dr Nirmala MBBS, DNB (Obs & Gynec) is an infertility specialist and practicing obstetrician for the last twenty years, running her own Test Tube Baby Hospital in Bangalore, for the last five years. She is the Managing and Clinical Director of her own IVF Centre in Bangalore named “ADVANCED FERTILITY CENTRE”. She is also a visiting consultant at various well known hospitals in and around Bangalore dealing with 70 – 80 patients, performing 4 -5 surgeries, 3 -4 day-care procedures per day along with emergency calls of delivery and Caesareans. . Her hospital also has the privilege of being Karnataka’s First test tube baby center who gave first VITRIFICATION (rapid freezing of embryo) baby. 

Dr. Nirmala also has submitted various papers and presentations at international conferences and contributed towards several medical books and journals. Recently, she has published a book “Stress and Infertility – A Holistic Approach”. “Meditation is helping my anguished patient couples to successful parenthood” says Dr. Nirmala with overflowing joy and gratitude. Amidst all this, she has taken time to complete FIMT and engages in teaching meditation to her patients. 

She has also performed “ Live interaction program on infertility” telecast on Udaya TV, Doordarshan, Ushe TV, C – Bangalore and E-TV., Suvarna, Ice T.V, Siti, Kasturi, Forum, TV – 9 etc.

Every day the Doctor makes it a point to spend a few hours serving the mission in various capacities. Every weekend she organizes and conducts a free medical camp at Bidadi while also running a mobile clinic, taking the much needed medical help to the 50 odd villages close by. 

Dr. Nirmala says, “Shouldering a few responsibilities, spending a few hours serving the mission gives me tremendous energy to run many more hours of my life effectively, “Thanks to My Master.” 

Dr. Nirmala's intensity and dedication in serving the mission and the society is something to learn from.
